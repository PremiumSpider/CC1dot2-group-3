Reptiles

Lizards are a group of reptiles that are characterized by their scaly skin and clawed feet. They come in various sizes and colors, and can be found in different habitats around the world.

Snakes are elongated, legless reptiles that are known for their ability to slither and their unique feeding mechanism. They can be venomous or non-venomous, and are found in a wide range of habitats.

Turtles are reptiles that have a protective shell and can retract their head and limbs into it. They are known for their slow movement and can be found both on land and in water.

Crocodiles are large, heavily armored reptiles with long snouts and powerful jaws capable of delivering a deadly bite. They are found throughout the tropics and can thrive in fresh or salt water environments where they patiently hunt fish, birds, and mammals.

Chameleons are a distinctive family of climbing lizards known for their ability to change color and the extraordinary speed and range of their long tongues, used to capture prey like insects and small vertebrates. Found in warm habitats ranging from rainforests to deserts, most chameleons have prehensile tails, feet suited to grasping branches, and eyes that can rotate independently.

The bearded dragon is a medium-sized lizard that is native to Australia and belongs to the genus Pogona. It has spiny scales around its throat that when puffed up resemble a beard, and can change color from light to dark to regulate body temperature or show mood.
